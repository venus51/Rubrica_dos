Barranquilla seawall

Description: The Barranquilla Malecón, officially called Gran Malecón del Río, is one of the most emblematic urban projects in the city. Opened in 2017, it aims to connect Barranquilla with the Magdalena River, recovering its historical relationship with water. Its construction revitalized a deteriorated industrial area, transforming it into a recreational and cultural space with green areas, restaurants, and events. Location: It is located on the western bank of Barranquilla, along the Magdalena River, near the city center and the port. It is easily accessible from main roads such as Avenida del Río.

Machu Pichuu

Description: Machu Picchu is an ancient Inca city located in the Andes Mountains of Peru. Built in the 15th century during the height of the Inca Empire, it was rediscovered in 1911 by explorer Hiram Bingham. Machu Picchu is famous for its impressive stone architecture, agricultural terraces, and spectacular mountain views. It is believed to have been an important religious, political, and administrative center. The city is situated at about 2,430 meters above sea level, on a plateau between the peaks of Huayna Picchu and Machu Picchu, giving it a strategic location and stunning landscapes.

Eiffel Tower

Description: The Eiffel Tower is one of the most iconic monuments in the world, located in Paris, France. It was designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel and built between 1887 and 1889 for the 1889 World's Fair, which commemorated the centenary of the French Revolution. Originally criticized by some intellectuals and artists of the time, the tower has become a symbol of innovation and modernity. The Eiffel Tower is located on the Champ de Mars, near the Seine River, in the 7th arrondissement of Paris, France. Its exact address is Champ de Mars, 5 Avenue Anatole France. It is easily accessible from several points in the city and close to other major attractions such as the Louvre Museum and the Arc de Triomphe.

the great wall of china

Description:The Great Wall of China is an ancient fortification built to protect the Chinese empire from invasions and nomadic attacks from the north. Its construction began in the 3rd century BC under Emperor Qin Shi Huang, although it was extended and reinforced during several dynasties, most notably during the Ming Dynasty (1368–1644). The wall is over 21,000 kilometres long, spanning mountains, deserts and plains. In addition to its military function, the Great Wall served to control borders and regulate trade along the Silk Road. Location: The wall stretches across northern China, from the Korean border in the east to the Gobi Desert in the west. Near Beijing, the most visited and restored sections are located.

christ of the redeemer

Description:Christ the Redeemer is a monumental statue of Jesus Christ located in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Inaugurated in 1931, the statue is 38 meters high, including its pedestal, and has its arms outstretched as a symbol of peace and welcome. It was designed by the French sculptor Paul Landowski and built by the Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa, with details on the face made by the Romanian sculptor Gheorghe Leonida. It is one of the most famous monuments in the world and a symbol of Christianity, as well as being an emblem of the city of Rio. Location: It is located at the top of Corcovado Hill, 710 meters above sea level, within the Tijuca National Park, from where it offers panoramic views of the city and its landscapes.
